# Baby Hippo Public Beta Review

Review date: June 22, 2026  
Review perspective: First-time visitor with limited Web3 knowledge  
Review scope: Public website only; no code changes were made

## Executive Summary

Baby Hippo already has a strong emotional identity, unusually responsible risk language, and a coherent mission for ordinary working people. It does not feel like a casino, trading terminal, or speculative meme project. That is the product's clearest advantage.

The main public-beta weakness is not missing functionality. It is the connection between pages:

- Users can read content without the Journey recognizing it.
- Users can claim completion without reading the content.
- Several CTAs lead to a broad page or a same-page teaser instead of the exact next step.
- The most important tools often appear below large decorative hero sections on mobile.
- Story, Learn, and Community have a visible mobile collision between the back link and page eyebrow.

The product is suitable for guided beta testing, but a truly self-guided beginner journey still needs clearer completion rules and stronger page-to-page handoffs.

**Overall first-time visitor score: 7.4 / 10**

## Review Method

The review covered:

- Desktop layout at the normal browser viewport.
- Mobile layout at 390 × 844.
- Traditional Chinese and English presentation.
- First-screen comprehension.
- Content hierarchy and page length.
- Primary and secondary CTAs.
- Beginner terminology and warnings.
- Journey and Points relationships.
- Navigation and visible interaction patterns.

The test began with a clean Journey state:

- 0 / 5 completed
- 0 BHP
- Traditional Chinese selected

External community services and third-party protocol documentation were not assessed beyond their visible links and context inside Baby Hippo.

## 1. Homepage

**Score: 8 / 10**

### What Is Good

- The positioning is warm, specific, and clearly different from ordinary crypto marketing.
- The slogan and founder background establish a memorable identity.
- The page consistently emphasizes education, risk management, honest work, and long-term growth.
- Audience cards make truck drivers, teachers, workers, rural communities, and small business owners feel intentionally included.
- Product cards explain Lobster Watch, Hippo Bank, and Hippo Academy without promising returns.
- The homepage contains strong safety language and avoids price speculation.
- The visual system feels cohesive and recognizably Baby Hippo.

### What Is Confusing

- The new Journey card appears before the main brand introduction. A first-time mobile visitor sees tasks, BHP, and Ether.fi before seeing what Baby Hippo is.
- The homepage contains several “Start Learning” CTAs, but some lead to the short homepage learning section rather than `/learn`.
- “Continue learning” inside the homepage lesson leads to the Join section, not the learning hub.
- “Hippo Bank” is described as a planning product, while the actual public navigation calls the tool DCA Planner.
- BHP appears before a first-time visitor receives a plain explanation that it is a local educational score.

### Missing Content

- A compact “What is Baby Hippo?” summary in one or two sentences before any achievement system.
- A visible link to the real Manifesto and Constitution; the homepage still says their public reading pages are coming.
- At least one real or clearly labeled sample Hippo Story.
- A simple “How the website works” path: Learn → Plan → Compare risk → Join.
- A short explanation of why BHP exists and why it has no monetary value.

### Missing CTA

- Hero “Start Learning” should lead directly to `/learn`.
- Hippo Bank should link directly to `/dca-planner`.
- Hippo Academy should link directly to `/learn`.
- The founder-story section should offer a direct `/story` CTA.
- The homepage lesson should offer a direct “Open Learning Hub” CTA.

### Mobile UX Problems

- The Journey card consumes essentially the entire first viewport.
- At 390 × 844, the main brand headline begins below the first screen, around 864 px from the page top.
- The total mobile page is approximately 8,900 px tall, creating substantial scrolling.
- Several supporting labels inside Journey and the footer are visually very small.
- The first visitor encounters five tasks before the brand promise, founder context, or safety philosophy.

### Beginner UX Problems

- Ether.fi and Aave appear in onboarding before the user understands staking, lending, or smart-contract risk.
- BHP can initially sound like a token despite the project being intentionally non-token in this flow.
- The distinction between homepage learning content, the Learn page, Hippo Academy, and Points is not immediately obvious.

## 2. Story

**Score: 8 / 10**

### What Is Good

- The founder story is the strongest emotional differentiator in the product.
- Logistics, freight work, violin teaching, rural roots, mistakes, and gradual learning feel honest rather than manufactured.
- The page admits uncertainty and mistakes instead of presenting the founder as a financial guru.
- The timeline gives the project a human narrative.
- “Founder lessons” and “Mistakes made” reinforce Baby Hippo's anti-hype positioning.
- Final CTAs to Learn and Community are appropriate.

### What Is Confusing

- The page is long enough that the core reason Baby Hippo exists can become diluted by repetition.
- References to DCA, Ether.fi, Aave, and DeFi appear before a true beginner has learned those concepts.
- The eight-part timeline is emotionally useful but does not clearly distinguish personal milestones from product milestones.

### Missing Content

- A short summary near the top: “This story explains why Baby Hippo was built.”
- Approximate dates or stages for key milestones, where the founder is comfortable sharing them.
- A brief boundary statement explaining that personal experience is not investment expertise.
- A closing recap with the three most important founder lessons.

### Missing CTA

- “Mark founder story as read” or another clear return path to Journey.
- A direct “Continue My Journey” CTA after the final founder statement.
- A visible link to the Baby Hippo Manifesto.

### Mobile UX Problems

- The back link and uppercase eyebrow visibly collide on the same line.
- The page is approximately 7,300 px tall on mobile.
- The opening headline is powerful, but its large size creates heavy wrapping.
- The long timeline can feel repetitive without a compact progress indicator.

### Beginner UX Problems

- A beginner may not understand why Ether.fi and Aave are part of the founder journey.
- The page does not offer inline definitions for the first use of DCA, DeFi, or staking-related terms.
- Reading the page does not automatically or clearly complete the related Points achievement.

## 3. Learn

**Score: 8 / 10**

### What Is Good

- The seven-topic curriculum is logically ordered.
- Each lesson includes:
  - A simple explanation
  - Why it matters
  - A beginner warning
  - A next step
- Wallet safety and risk management receive real emphasis.
- The tone is calm and does not pressure users to purchase anything.
- Next-step links create a continuous reading path.
- Bitcoin, Ethereum, DCA, Aave, Ether.fi, risk management, and seed phrases cover the right MVP foundations.

### What Is Confusing

- Seven lessons on one page create a long reading experience rather than a clear lesson-completion experience.
- “Complete Bitcoin lesson” exists in Points, but the lesson page itself has no completion control or confirmation.
- The relationship between Learn, Hippo Academy, Journey, and Points is not stated.
- “DCA” appears alongside its Chinese explanation, but the abbreviation can still feel like insider language.

### Missing Content

- Estimated reading time for each lesson.
- A mini glossary for:
  - Blockchain
  - Smart contract
  - Collateral
  - Liquidation
  - Staking
  - Restaking
  - Protocol
- One simple knowledge check per lesson.
- Lesson completion status.
- A compact “What should I learn first?” recommendation.

### Missing CTA

- “Mark lesson complete” at the end of each lesson.
- “Return to My Journey” after completion.
- A direct CTA from the DCA lesson to `/dca-planner`.
- Direct CTAs from Aave and Ether.fi lessons to the corresponding Earn comparison sections.

### Mobile UX Problems

- The back link and “Hippo Academy · Beginner Path” eyebrow visibly overlap.
- The page is approximately 7,700 px tall.
- There is no sticky lesson progress or compact lesson navigator.
- Reaching a specific later lesson requires significant scrolling unless the user returns to the learning-path links.

### Beginner UX Problems

- Aave and Ether.fi are still conceptually dense even with good explanations.
- The page explains concepts, but it does not test whether the user understood the safety implications.
- A user can claim Points without reading, while reading does not clearly grant Points.

## 4. DCA Planner

**Score: 7 / 10**

### What Is Good

- The opening principle—take care of real life first—is excellent.
- Income, fixed expenses, flexible expenses, and emergency cash are more responsible inputs than a typical crypto calculator.
- Calculations update immediately.
- Allocation percentages and dollar amounts are visible.
- The planner includes 1-, 3-, 5-, and 10-year illustrations.
- The page clearly states that projections are educational and not financial advice.
- Yield risk, emergency cash, and timing pressure receive appropriate warnings.
- Data remains local.

### What Is Confusing

- Input currency is not stated beside the fields. A Taiwan-based user may not know whether `5000` means USD, TWD, or another currency.
- “Emergency cash target” does not ask how much emergency cash the user already has.
- The model divides the entire target across 12 months, which can overstate the cash gap for someone who has already saved part of it.
- The fixed 4%, 5%, and 6% assumptions appear late in the page.
- The basis for those returns is not explained.
- “Yield” is presented like an asset beside BTC, ETH, SOL, and LINK even though it is a strategy category.
- Default values may be interpreted as recommended personal finances.

### Missing Content

- Currency selector or an explicit currency label.
- Current emergency cash balance.
- Target completion date or number of months.
- Investment horizon and goal.
- Transparent per-asset return assumptions.
- Scenario-based conservative, base, and optimistic projections.
- Clear explanation of fees, inflation, and cash drag.
- The planned Cycle Planner concepts documented for version 3.0.

### Missing CTA

- “Save this plan” confirmation, even if it remains local-only.
- “Mark DCA plan created” or automatic Journey completion.
- “Return to My Journey.”
- A direct follow-up to Learn or Earn based on the selected assets.

### Mobile UX Problems

- A large decorative projection card fills much of the first screen, pushing the actual calculator inputs down.
- The page is approximately 5,500 px tall on mobile.
- The hero headline wraps into many large lines.
- Help text under input fields is very small.
- Users must scroll considerably before reaching the calculation workspace.

### Beginner UX Problems

- Conservative, Balanced, and Growth profiles require more explanation about downside, not only allocation differences.
- A beginner may assume the projection line represents an expected outcome.
- The planner does not explain why SOL, LINK, or Yield should be selected.
- The emergency-fund formula is responsible but not transparent enough at the moment of input.

## 5. Earn

**Score: 7 / 10**

### What Is Good

- “Compare risk before exploring yield” is exactly the right framing.
- The page states that APR is not live and does not rank protocols.
- Ether.fi, Aave, HyperLend, and Kamino are compared using consistent questions.
- Main risks, exit difficulty, ecosystem, suitability, and yield source are included.
- Filters make the comparison more useful.
- The warning section is strong and clearly says DeFi is not a bank deposit.
- No wallet connection or transaction capability is present.

### What Is Confusing

- The “Beginner friendly” filter returns Aave even though its own description says supplying is intermediate and borrowing is not beginner-level.
- “Higher risk” includes three of four protocols, so it does not help a beginner distinguish degrees of risk.
- Risk labels do not explain the scoring method.
- Ether.fi and Aave Journey tasks both lead to the top of `/earn`, not directly to their protocol sections.
- “Educational data” does not display a review date or version.

### Missing Content

- A short glossary for lending, liquid staking, restaking, vault, collateral, and liquidity.
- A visible methodology for “Beginner suitability” and “Estimated risk.”
- “Last reviewed” date for each protocol.
- Clear protocol anchors for direct linking.
- A short “Start here if you are new” recommendation that favors learning rather than depositing.
- A warning that protocol documentation may be technical and primarily English.

### Missing CTA

- “I reviewed Ether.fi” and “I reviewed Aave” completion actions.
- “Return to My Journey.”
- Direct “Learn this concept first” links from each protocol card.
- A neutral “Compare two protocols” action would be more useful than only official-resource links.

### Mobile UX Problems

- The decorative protocol-orbit graphic pushes the filters below the first screen.
- The page is approximately 7,000 px tall.
- The filter row uses horizontal scrolling with a visible browser-style scrollbar.
- Protocol cards contain a large amount of dense text.
- The comparison table requires horizontal movement and is difficult to scan on a phone.
- This page had roughly 41 visible text elements below 9 px in the mobile layout audit.

### Beginner UX Problems

- Four protocols and several risk dimensions are presented before teaching the comparison vocabulary.
- Official documentation is likely too technical to be the primary next step for many beginners.
- “Yield source” can still sound like passive income without an immediate explanation of principal risk.

## 6. Community

**Score: 8 / 10**

### What Is Good

- The mission is welcoming and clearly aimed at ordinary people.
- Telegram and X CTAs are visible immediately.
- Safety copy warns that Baby Hippo will never request seed phrases or private keys.
- Community values and expected behavior are presented before the final join section.
- The page clearly names the people the community is for.
- The tone is inclusive rather than exclusive or status-driven.

### What Is Confusing

- A first-time visitor may not know the difference between joining Telegram and following X.
- The page does not clearly explain what happens after joining.
- Journey says “Join Community,” while Points specifically uses “Join Telegram.”
- Clicking an external community link does not visibly complete the Journey task.

### Missing Content

- Community schedule or expected activity.
- Clear channel purpose:
  - Telegram for conversation
  - X for public updates
- Moderation and reporting process.
- Community rules or Code of Conduct link.
- Official contact method.
- Privacy reminder about what financial information members should never share publicly.

### Missing CTA

- “I joined the community” or a safe completion confirmation.
- “Return to My Journey.”
- “Read community values” as a distinct action connected to Points.
- A direct invitation to introduce oneself using a safe, non-financial prompt.

### Mobile UX Problems

- The back link and “People Are the Community” eyebrow overlap.
- The page is approximately 5,300 px tall.
- Telegram and X CTAs are clear, but the explanatory distinction between them appears later.
- The hero art adds significant vertical length after the main CTA area.

### Beginner UX Problems

- New users need reassurance about what questions are welcome.
- The page should explicitly say never to post balances, wallet recovery information, or private financial details.
- There is no simple description of the first safe community action after joining.

## 7. Points

**Score: 7 / 10**

### What Is Good

- The non-token disclaimer is prominent and explicit.
- Total points, level, completed achievements, and category progress are easy to understand.
- Learning, Planning, Yield, and Community create a coherent product journey.
- Local-only storage is clearly disclosed.
- Each achievement has a related-page link.
- Reset requires confirmation.
- The design feels educational rather than speculative.

### What Is Confusing

- “BHP” resembles a crypto ticker even though the page says it is not a token.
- Completion is manual, so users can claim achievements without completing them.
- Reading a lesson does not automatically update Points.
- Journey and Points display the same achievement data, but this relationship is not explained on the Points page.
- The current MVP offers 210 points, making Level 3 and Level 4 unreachable.
- The explanation that higher levels are reserved for future achievements appears after users see the inaccessible levels.

### Missing Content

- Exact completion criteria for each achievement.
- A distinction between verified, self-confirmed, and future automatically detected achievements.
- A short explanation of what Points are useful for today.
- A clearer explanation of why Levels 3 and 4 are visible now.
- A “recommended next achievement” near the dashboard.

### Missing CTA

- A prominent next recommended action.
- “Return to My Journey.”
- More descriptive related-page links; every task currently says “前往相關頁面.”
- A filtered view for “Journey tasks only.”

### Mobile UX Problems

- The large decorative hero graphic delays the actual achievement dashboard.
- The page is approximately 6,800 px tall.
- Eighteen achievement cards create a long, cognitively heavy list.
- Many labels and helper lines are very small; the audit found roughly 60 visible text elements below 9 px.
- The two-column summary works, but supporting text is difficult to read.

### Beginner UX Problems

- BHP needs a plain-language name before its acronym.
- Yield achievements appear alongside beginner lessons without a prerequisite signal.
- Users may interpret higher levels as promised future rewards despite the disclaimer.
- The difference between completing a lesson and simply checking a box is unclear.

## 8. My Journey

**Score: 6 / 10**

My Journey is reviewed separately because it is the main onboarding experience, even though it appears inside the homepage rather than at a separate route.

### What Is Good

- Five tasks are much more approachable than the full eighteen-achievement Points page.
- The progress bar and 0 / 5 count are immediately understandable.
- “Next Recommended Action” removes choice paralysis.
- Tasks connect Learn, DCA Planner, Earn, Community, and Points.
- Traditional Chinese and English are both supported.
- Progress persists locally.
- Journey and Points use the same underlying achievement IDs and totals.
- Completion produces immediate visual feedback and a checkmark.

### What Is Confusing

- Clicking the main task row marks it complete; clicking the small chevron navigates to the task. Many users will expect the whole row to open the lesson.
- Users can mark a task complete before visiting the related page.
- Visiting or finishing the related page does not automatically mark the task complete.
- Ether.fi and Aave both lead to the same general Earn page instead of their exact guides.
- The card shows total BHP from the entire Points system, not only the five Journey tasks. A user could have 0 / 5 Journey progress but nonzero BHP.
- “Join Community” maps specifically to the Telegram achievement, but that detail is not shown.

### Missing Content

- One sentence explaining how completion works.
- A distinction between “Open task” and “Mark complete.”
- Exact completion criteria for each task.
- A confirmation step after returning from a task.
- A final recommended action after 5 / 5, such as reviewing all achievements or revisiting risk lessons.
- Prerequisite context before introducing Ether.fi and Aave.

### Missing CTA

- A single unambiguous primary CTA per task.
- Direct Ether.fi and Aave anchor links.
- “Continue where I left off” after returning to the homepage.
- “Return to Journey” links from every destination page.
- A final “Explore all achievements” CTA after completion should be more visually prominent.

### Mobile UX Problems

- The card fills almost the complete first viewport.
- The main Baby Hippo headline and mission begin below the fold.
- Five rows, two actions per row, a points counter, progress, next action, and footer note make the first screen busy.
- The chevron-only navigation action is visually ambiguous.
- Points and local-storage helper text are very small.

### Beginner UX Problems

- The user is asked to understand Points before understanding Baby Hippo.
- Ether.fi and Aave arrive too early in a beginner's first five steps.
- The system rewards self-reporting but does not clearly tell the user when it is honest to check a task.
- “Create DCA Plan” can be marked complete without saving or confirming a plan.

## Cross-Page Findings

### What the Public Beta Does Especially Well

1. **Trustworthy voice**  
   The site consistently avoids guaranteed-return language.

2. **Strong founder-market fit**  
   The rural Miaoli, logistics, freight, and music-teaching background gives the product a reason to exist.

3. **Risk before action**  
   Seed phrase safety, emergency cash, liquidation, smart-contract risk, and protocol risk are visible.

4. **Consistent visual identity**  
   The dark blue-gray, amber, teal, and Baby Hippo mascot direction feel cohesive.

5. **Real beginner intent**  
   The product repeatedly explains that ordinary people deserve access to understandable tools.

### Most Important Beta Risks

1. **Journey completion can be claimed without completing the work.**
2. **Completing the work does not automatically update Journey or Points.**
3. **The homepage shows gamification before explaining the product.**
4. **Story, Learn, and Community have a mobile breadcrumb/eyebrow collision.**
5. **Several mobile pages delay useful content behind decorative hero graphics.**
6. **DCA assumptions and financial input meanings are not transparent enough.**
7. **Earn and Points are text-dense and use very small supporting copy.**

## Recommended Priorities

These are recommendations only; no code was changed during this review.

### Priority 0 — Before Broad Public Beta

1. Fix the mobile back-link and eyebrow collision on Story, Learn, and Community.
2. Clarify Journey interactions:
   - The primary row action should open the task.
   - Completion should be a separate, clearly labeled action.
3. Add direct Journey return links to Story, Learn, DCA Planner, Earn, and Community.
4. Link Ether.fi and Aave Journey tasks to exact protocol sections.
5. Explain self-confirmed completion before users can award themselves Points.

### Priority 1 — Improve First-Time Conversion

1. Let the homepage brand promise appear before or alongside Journey on mobile.
2. Send homepage learning CTAs directly to `/learn`.
3. Send Hippo Bank directly to `/dca-planner`.
4. Add lesson completion and Journey synchronization.
5. Add currency and current emergency-cash inputs to DCA Planner.
6. Move projection assumptions closer to DCA results.
7. Reduce or collapse decorative hero art on mobile for DCA, Earn, and Points.

### Priority 2 — Improve Understanding and Retention

1. Add a shared beginner glossary.
2. Add reading-time estimates and knowledge checks.
3. Explain Points levels and currently achievable maximum earlier.
4. Add Earn risk methodology and review dates.
5. Explain Telegram versus X before the Community CTAs.
6. Add community conduct, moderation, privacy, and onboarding expectations.

## Final Recommendation

Baby Hippo is ready for a **small, guided public beta** with founder involvement.

It is not yet fully ready for an entirely self-guided beginner audience because the onboarding completion model is ambiguous and several cross-page handoffs are incomplete.

The right beta question is not “Do users like the design?” The design is already distinctive.

The most valuable questions are:

- Can a first-time visitor explain what Baby Hippo does after 30 seconds?
- Do users know whether to open or complete a Journey task?
- Can they find their way back after reading a lesson?
- Do they understand why BHP is not a token?
- Can they explain the assumptions behind the DCA projection?
- Can they compare protocol risk without feeling encouraged to deposit?

If those questions test well after the Priority 0 fixes, Baby Hippo will have a much stronger foundation for broader public beta growth.

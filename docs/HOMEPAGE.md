# Baby Hippo Homepage Specification

> **Audience:** First-time visitors with little or no Web3 experience  
> **Primary goal:** Explain Baby Hippo within 30 seconds  
> **Primary actions:** Join the community or start learning

## Homepage Promise

A first-time visitor should quickly understand:

1. Baby Hippo is an on-chain growth community for ordinary people.
2. It was created from real experience in rural Miaoli, logistics, freight work, and music education.
3. Its first priorities are learning, risk management, and steady progress.
4. It does not promise easy money or promote token speculation.
5. There is a clear, beginner-friendly way to participate.

## 30-Second Visitor Journey

| Time | Visitor should understand |
|---:|---|
| 0–5 seconds | “This community is for people like me.” |
| 5–10 seconds | “It comes from a real founder story.” |
| 10–18 seconds | “The products help me understand risk, learn, and plan.” |
| 18–25 seconds | “Real community members matter more than token hype.” |
| 25–30 seconds | “I can join or begin learning without buying anything.” |

## Recommended Header

### Desktop

- Baby Hippo logo and name on the left
- Navigation: Our Story, Community, Products, Hippo Stories, Values
- Language selector: 中文 / EN
- Primary button: **Join Community**

### Mobile

- Logo and short name
- Language selector
- Menu button
- Keep one visible CTA: **Join Community**

Do not place token price, market chart, wallet balance, or “Buy BHC” in the header.

# Section 2 — Our Story

## Purpose

Show that Baby Hippo comes from lived experience rather than a marketing trend.

## Recommended Heading

> Built From Real Life  
> 從真實生活出發

## Recommended Founder Copy

> Baby Hippo began in a rural town in Miaoli, Taiwan.
>
> The founder has worked in logistics and freight transportation and teaches violin. He understands long hours on the road, the responsibilities carried by working families, and the patience required to keep grassroots education alive.
>
> While learning about DeFi, wallets, and risk management, he saw how difficult the on-chain world can feel to ordinary people. The language is complicated. The risks are often hidden. Too many projects begin with hype instead of education.
>
> Baby Hippo was created to build a different path: clear learning, practical risk tools, steady habits, and a community where beginners are treated with respect.

## Founder Quote

> “I am not building Baby Hippo for people who already know everything. I am building it for people who work hard, want to learn, and deserve a fair place to begin.”

## Layout Recommendation

Use a two-column layout:

- Left: Founder or Miaoli-inspired image
- Right: Short story, founder quote, and **Read Our Manifesto** link

Do not make this section a long biography. The visitor should understand the origin in three short paragraphs.

## Content Safety

- Use genuine founder images only with permission.
- Clearly label illustrations or recreated scenes.
- Avoid presenting hardship as a branding prop.
- Do not imply that the founder's personal learning path guarantees outcomes for others.

# Content and Design Direction

## Tone

The homepage should feel:

- Friendly rather than technical
- Founder-led rather than corporate
- Community-focused rather than token-focused
- Patient rather than urgent
- Hopeful without promising outcomes
- Clear enough for a first-time Web3 visitor

## Visual Language

Recommended:

- Warm earth, river, field, and sky colors
- Friendly rounded shapes
- Documentary photography or respectful illustration
- Real workplaces, instruments, roads, shops, and rural landscapes
- A baby hippo character used as a gentle guide
- Large readable type and generous spacing

Avoid:

- Candlestick charts in the hero
- Rockets, moons, casinos, piles of money, or luxury cars
- Neon hacker imagery
- Meme-coin visual language
- Countdown timers
- Fake scarcity or fear-of-missing-out messages
- Large token symbols dominating the page

## Language Strategy

- Show Traditional Chinese and English.
- The first rendered language should follow browser preference where possible.
- Keep headings short in both languages.
- Do not mix unexplained abbreviations into introductory copy.
- Terms such as DCA, DeFi, APY, collateral, and liquidation should have plain-language definitions or tooltips.

## Accessibility

- Meet WCAG AA color contrast.
- Use readable text sizes and visible focus states.
- Give every image meaningful alternative text.
- Do not communicate risk using color alone.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Make all actions usable by keyboard.
- Use descriptive link labels rather than repeated “Learn More.”
- Ensure emoji have accompanying text labels.

## Performance

- Prioritize fast loading on older phones and mobile networks.
- Compress images and avoid autoplay video.
- Render the hero message without waiting for wallet or blockchain services.
- The homepage should remain useful if market-data services are unavailable.

# CTA and Measurement Plan

## Primary CTA Hierarchy

1. Join Community
2. Start Learning
3. Explore a product
4. Read a story
5. Read the Manifesto or Constitution
6. Share Your Story

Wallet connection should not be the homepage's primary CTA.

## Recommended Events

- Hero community CTA selected
- Hero learning CTA selected
- Founder story viewed
- Audience card selected
- Product card selected
- Hippo Story opened
- Manifesto opened
- Constitution opened
- Final community CTA selected
- Language changed

Do not use engagement tracking to infer sensitive financial status. Collect only what is needed to improve clarity and usability.

## Homepage Success Checks

After viewing the homepage, a visitor should be able to answer:

- What is Baby Hippo?
- Who is it for?
- Why did the founder create it?
- What are its four core products?
- Does it promise financial returns?
- What can I do first without buying anything?

Test these questions with drivers, workers, teachers, small business owners, rural residents, and first-time Web3 learners before launch.

# Copy Guardrails

## Approved Direction

- “Learn at your own pace.”
- “Understand the risk.”
- “Build steady habits.”
- “Testnet learning reward.”
- “No promised monetary value.”
- “Personal experience, not a guaranteed result.”
- “You do not have to know everything to begin.”

## Do Not Use

- “Guaranteed returns”
- “Risk-free yield”
- “Get rich”
- “Next 100x”
- “Don't miss out”
- “Buy before it is too late”
- “Passive income guaranteed”
- “AI-protected profits”
- “The next meme coin”
- Any language implying that joining the community requires buying BHC

# Founder Review Checklist

Before approving the homepage, ask:

- Can a non-technical visitor understand Baby Hippo within 30 seconds?
- Does the founder story feel real rather than manufactured?
- Are ordinary people visible before products and tokens?
- Is Lobster Watch clearly the first product priority?
- Can someone start learning without connecting a wallet?
- Is BHC clearly described as a testnet learning reward?
- Are Manifesto and Constitution links easy to find?
- Are Hippo Stories presented with dignity and consent?
- Is every financial limitation stated honestly?
- Would we feel comfortable showing this page to a driver, worker, teacher, or family member face to face?

# Baby Hippo Brand Identity

> **From Worker To On-Chain Boss**  
> **從上班族，成為鏈上老闆**

## Purpose of This System

This document defines how Baby Hippo should look, sound, and feel across products, community spaces, education, social media, events, merchandise, and future campaigns.

Baby Hippo is not only a truck driver story. The brand grows from five connected worlds:

1. **Logistics and transportation** — honest work, endurance, responsibility, and roads traveled.
2. **Music and arts education** — patience, practice, teaching, creativity, and human expression.
3. **Rural hometown roots** — Miaoli, mountains, fields, local communities, and grounded values.
4. **Web3 learning** — wallets, digital ownership, DeFi literacy, and practical technology.
5. **Long-term wealth building** — education, discipline, saving habits, risk management, and gradual progress without guaranteed outcomes.

The visual identity should make these worlds feel like parts of one life—not unrelated decorations assembled around a token.

## Six Brand Pillars

Every visual, message, product, and community experience should express at least two of these pillars. Major brand work should express all six as one coherent system.

### 1. Honest Work

We respect the dignity of people who earn, teach, build, drive, repair, serve, and care through real effort. Workwear, roads, tools, and practical details represent this pillar.

### 2. Education

Knowledge should be understandable and shareable. Books, music practice, lesson boards, instruments, and patient explanation represent this pillar.

### 3. Discipline

Progress comes from repeated practice, safer habits, planning, and the willingness to continue. Routes, rhythm, milestones, and steady visual patterns represent this pillar.

### 4. Technology

Wallets, DeFi, and blockchain tools should feel useful rather than mysterious. Connected pathways, clear interfaces, hardware wallets, and restrained digital nodes represent this pillar.

### 5. Hope

Baby Hippo believes ordinary people can build greater capability and a better future without pretending the road is easy. Morning light, open horizons, and growing landscapes represent this pillar.

### 6. Community

People matter more than tokens. Groups, shared learning, local places, conversation, and Hippo Stories represent this pillar.

## One-Sentence Creative Test

> Does this work make honest effort, learning, discipline, useful technology, realistic hope, and community belonging feel like parts of the same journey?

# 1. Brand Story

Baby Hippo began from real life in a rural town in Miaoli, Taiwan.

Its founder has worked in logistics and freight transportation and teaches violin. One world is built around roads, schedules, physical work, and responsibility. The other is built around listening, patience, practice, and helping students grow. Both require discipline. Both are rarely celebrated by financial technology.

While learning about wallets, DeFi, and risk management, the founder saw a familiar gap: ordinary people are expected to enter complicated systems without enough time, education, or protection. Too much of crypto speaks to traders, whales, and insiders. Too little is designed for a driver between routes, a teacher between lessons, a worker after a long shift, or a family in a rural community.

Baby Hippo brings those experiences together.

It is an on-chain growth community where hard work meets education, hometown roots meet modern technology, and long-term discipline replaces short-term hype.

The brand should always communicate:

> We respect where people begin.  
> We help them understand the road ahead.  
> We grow one responsible step at a time.

## Brand Positioning

**Primary position**

> An on-chain growth community built for everyone working hard to improve life.

**Traditional Chinese**

> 為每一位努力生活的人打造的鏈上成長社群。

**Brand promise**

> Clear education, practical risk awareness, and a community that values steady progress.

This is a promise about how Baby Hippo will serve people. It is not a promise of financial performance.

## The Five-World Story

| World | Human meaning | Visual expression |
|---|---|---|
| Logistics | Hard work and responsibility | Roads, truck-cab shapes, reflective details |
| Music education | Patience and learning | Violin curves, piano rhythm, musical notes |
| Rural roots | Belonging and grounded hope | Mountains, fields, sunrise, local roads |
| Web3 learning | Access to modern tools | Nodes, pathways, wallet symbols, subtle grids |
| Long-term building | Discipline and gradual progress | Horizons, milestones, growing paths, rings |

No single world should dominate every application. The system should combine them according to context.

# 2. Brand Personality

## Core Traits

### Grounded

Baby Hippo speaks from lived experience. It does not pretend to be more glamorous, wealthy, or certain than it is.

### Warm

Beginners should feel welcomed rather than tested. The brand explains without talking down to people.

### Dependable

Like a careful driver, teacher, or worker, Baby Hippo checks the details, shows the risks, and does not take shortcuts with trust.

### Patient

Practice, DCA habits, risk awareness, and community trust take time. The brand never creates artificial urgency.

### Curious

Baby Hippo is willing to learn new tools while asking practical questions: What does this do? What can go wrong? Who does it help?

### Hopeful

The brand believes ordinary people can understand technology and build a better future. Hope should feel earned and realistic, never magical.

### Quietly Confident

Sunglasses give the mascot a little attitude, but the confidence comes from preparation and discipline—not swagger, dominance, or showing off wealth.

## Personality Balance

| More like | Less like |
|---|---|
| A trusted teacher | A market guru |
| A reliable road companion | A loud promoter |
| A patient coach | A high-pressure salesperson |
| A skilled worker | A luxury influencer |
| A curious beginner-friendly guide | A technical gatekeeper |
| A local community builder | A faceless crypto corporation |

## Emotional Outcome

After meeting the brand, a person should feel:

- “This was built with people like me in mind.”
- “I can ask a basic question here.”
- “I should understand the risk before I act.”
- “Progress does not have to happen overnight.”
- “Technology can be practical, not intimidating.”

## Brand Archetype

### Primary Archetype — The Caregiver

Baby Hippo protects, welcomes, and helps people avoid preventable harm. This archetype is visible in Lobster Watch, wallet-safety education, patient onboarding, and the promise to put community before token price.

The Caregiver should feel capable and respectful—not parental, controlling, or overprotective.

### Secondary Archetype — The Sage

Baby Hippo believes understanding should come before action. It translates complex ideas into plain language, shows evidence, and admits uncertainty.

The Sage should feel accessible—not academic, superior, or full of jargon.

### Supporting Archetype — The Everyperson

Baby Hippo belongs with drivers, workers, teachers, small businesses, rural families, and first-time learners. The brand does not perform exclusivity.

The Everyperson should feel dignified and specific—not generic or cheaply “relatable.”

### Archetype Formula

> **The Caregiver protects the community.**  
> **The Sage helps it understand.**  
> **The Everyperson reminds us whom we serve.**

### Archetypes We Reject

- **The Ruler:** status, domination, exclusivity, and wealth as authority
- **The Outlaw:** exploitation, hacking glamour, and rule-breaking as identity
- **The Magician:** impossible transformation and promises of effortless wealth
- **The Jester when misused:** meme-first communication that trivializes risk

# 3. Visual and Illustration Style

## Creative Direction

The visual world is **rural workwear meets music classroom meets approachable technology**.

It should combine:

- Rounded, friendly character design
- Practical workwear textures and reflective bands
- Warm natural landscapes
- Musical rhythm and elegant instrument curves
- Clean digital pathways and simple interface geometry
- A calm sense of forward motion

The style should feel illustrated, human, and contemporary—not childish, corporate, or hyper-futuristic.

## Recommended Visual Ratio

For a typical brand composition:

- 40% human warmth and community
- 25% work and transportation
- 15% music and education
- 10% rural landscape
- 10% Web3 or digital pathways

This is a creative guideline, not a mathematical rule. It prevents blockchain graphics or truck imagery from overpowering the human mission.

## Shape Language

### Primary shapes

- Rounded rectangles
- Soft arches
- Broad curves inspired by a hippo's body
- Road lines that gently move toward a horizon
- Concentric rings suggesting practice and compounding

### Secondary shapes

- Violin f-holes and instrument curves
- Piano-key rhythms
- Reflective safety stripes
- Mountain silhouettes
- Connected digital nodes

### Avoid

- Aggressive spikes
- Sharp metallic shields
- Flames
- Explosions
- Weapon-like forms
- Casino chips
- Oversized coins
- Charts shooting vertically upward

## Illustration Style

Recommended:

- Clean editorial illustration
- Soft dimensional shading
- Slightly textured, tactile surfaces
- Friendly proportions
- Expressive but simple facial features
- Natural daylight, sunrise, or early-evening warmth
- Clear visual hierarchy suitable for mobile screens

Avoid:

- Photorealistic mascot renderings that feel uncanny
- Overly infantile cartoon style
- Highly glossy 3D crypto advertising
- Dark cyberpunk environments
- Neon hacker rooms
- Generic stock-photo luxury

## Photography Direction

When using real people:

- Photograph people in real environments with informed consent.
- Show dignity, skill, concentration, and everyday life.
- Include roads, classrooms, workshops, shops, instruments, and landscapes.
- Prefer natural light and honest color.
- Let the person look capable, not pitied.
- Avoid staged displays of cash, token balances, or trading screens.

## Graphic Motif: The Learning Road

The main supporting graphic motif is a road that gradually becomes a digital pathway.

It can begin as:

- A rural road
- A freight route
- A music staff
- A row of piano keys

It then becomes:

- Connected nodes
- A safe route through risk markers
- A pathway toward a calm horizon

This motif unites the founder story without forcing every design to show a truck.

## Illustration Composition System

Use three visual depths to combine the brand worlds without clutter:

1. **Human foreground:** Mascot or real community member doing something understandable.
2. **Life middle ground:** Truck cab, classroom, shop, instrument, workshop, or local road.
3. **Future background:** Mountains, fields, horizon, and restrained blockchain pathways.

Music should often appear as rhythm and shape rather than floating decoration. Piano keys can become a road edge. A violin curve can frame a portrait. Musical notes can transition into connected learning nodes.

## Visual Reference Board

Future designers should gather references from these categories:

### Honest Work

- Reflective safety vests and silver bands
- Freight-route markings
- Truck-cab materials and dashboard geometry
- Work gloves, notebooks, thermos flasks, and route maps

### Arts Education

- Violin wood, curves, bridges, strings, and f-holes
- Piano-key spacing and black-and-ivory rhythm
- Music stands, rehearsal notes, classrooms, and instrument cases
- Quiet practice rather than concert-stage glamour

### Rural Roots

- Miaoli-inspired layered mountains
- Local roads, fields, morning mist, small shops, and community rooms
- Natural materials, weathered signs, wood, canvas, and paper
- Hometown warmth without romanticizing hardship

### Useful Technology

- Hardware wallets
- Simple educational dashboards
- Nodes connected like routes rather than speculative energy
- Calm data visualization and clear status symbols

### Long-Term Growth

- Sunrise and distant horizons
- Repeated practice marks
- Route milestones
- Tree rings, field rows, and gradual upward paths

Reference gathering must avoid copying a single artist's recognizable style. Build mood boards around materials, composition, lighting, and meaning.

## Website Visual Style

### Overall Experience

The website should feel like entering a welcoming community learning center beside a well-traveled road: grounded, clear, calm, and gently optimistic.

### Page Rhythm

- Use generous cream or ivory space.
- Alternate quiet editorial sections with illustrated community moments.
- Let roads, piano-key rhythms, or digital paths guide the eye between sections.
- Keep token-related content visually smaller than education, risk, and community content.
- Use rounded cards with sturdy borders rather than glassy speculative dashboards.

### Hero

- Wide editorial scene with clear space for bilingual copy.
- Mascot and community appear as guides, not sales characters.
- Use warm daylight and a visible horizon.
- Primary CTA uses Workwear Amber with Deep Road Navy text.
- Avoid token price, wallet connection, charts, and countdowns above the fold.

### Product Sections

- Lobster Watch: evidence-led risk cards with icons and plain-language labels.
- Hippo Bank: route and milestone visuals for DCA planning.
- Hippo Academy: classroom, practice, and music-inspired learning visuals.
- BHC Rewards: badge-like progress markers rather than shiny coin imagery.
- Hippo Stories: portrait-led editorial cards with human quotes.

### Interface Details

- Use 12–20 px rounded corners depending on component size.
- Keep shadows soft and shallow.
- Prefer line icons with rounded ends.
- Use subtle textures inspired by paper, canvas, reflective material, and violin wood.
- Animation should resemble a route being drawn, a lesson progressing, or nodes gently connecting.

### Website Background Motifs

- Faint road center lines
- Piano-key section dividers
- Mountain silhouettes
- Violin-curve corner flourishes
- Sparse teal blockchain nodes

Use only one or two motifs per section. Empty space is part of the identity.

### Website Anti-Patterns

- Black trading-terminal pages
- Neon gradients and glowing coins
- Excessive glassmorphism
- Constant motion
- Price tickers as decoration
- Green “profit” buttons
- Large wallet-connect prompts before education
- Mascot poses that pressure users to act

# 4. Mascot Design

## Core Character

The Baby Hippo mascot is a friendly blue-gray hippo who represents hard work, education, discipline, technology, long-term growth, and hope.

The mascot should feel like:

- A reliable coworker
- A patient learning companion
- A calm protector
- A person who has done the work before speaking
- Someone with gentle humor and earned confidence

It should not feel like a celebrity trader, gangster, billionaire, hacker, or casino host.

## Required Physical Design

### Body

- Blue-gray skin
- Rounded, sturdy silhouette
- Slightly youthful proportions without appearing like a baby toy
- Friendly muzzle and small rounded ears
- Calm smile or focused expression

### Reflective Work Vest

- High-visibility amber or warm yellow
- Silver reflective bands
- Simple, practical cut
- No fake corporate logos or financial slogans

The vest represents honest labor, safety, preparedness, and respect for working people.

### Sunglasses

- Dark navy or smoke-gray lenses
- Rounded rectangular shape
- Modest scale
- A subtle reflection of road, mountains, or digital nodes may be used

The sunglasses add personality. They should communicate calm confidence, not arrogance.

### Hardware Wallet

- The mascot holds a Ledger hardware wallet or a clearly recognizable hardware-wallet form.
- The device must not display a seed phrase, private key, PIN, balance, or promotional price.
- When the Ledger trademark or exact product design is visible in public commercial artwork, confirm current trademark and brand-use permissions.
- An unbranded hardware-wallet silhouette should be available when third-party branding is inappropriate.

The wallet represents self-custody education and careful use of technology—not wealth.

### Semi-Truck Setting

- The mascot sits inside a clean, grounded semi-truck cab.
- The truck must be parked when the mascot is holding or looking at the hardware wallet.
- Seatbelt, steering wheel, mirrors, dashboard, and work details can support authenticity.
- The cab should feel like a real workplace, not a luxury vehicle.

Never show the mascot operating a wallet, phone, or learning app while driving.

### Driving and Wallet Safety Variants

The brand may show that the mascot is a semi-truck driver, but it must not combine distracted driving with wallet use.

- **Driving variant:** Truck in motion, seatbelt on, sunglasses on, both hands focused on driving. The hardware wallet is stored securely and not being handled.
- **Learning variant:** Truck parked safely at a rest area, engine off, mascot holding the hardware wallet while studying or checking a lesson.
- **Hero narrative variant:** The parked cab and road outside imply the driver's life while allowing the hardware wallet to be shown safely.

When a single image must include both the truck-driving identity and the Ledger, use the parked hero narrative variant.

## Required Background Elements

The full hero illustration should include:

- A subtle violin silhouette
- Piano keys
- Floating or path-following musical notes
- A rural landscape
- Mountains inspired by Miaoli
- Digital blockchain pathways or connected nodes

These should form one coherent scene.

### Recommended composition

- Foreground: Mascot seated in the parked semi-truck cab, holding the hardware wallet
- Middle ground: Dashboard or window framing with subtle piano-key rhythm
- Background: Rural fields, mountains, and a winding road
- Atmospheric layer: Violin silhouette and restrained musical notes
- Digital layer: Soft blue or teal nodes following the road toward the horizon

The blockchain pathways should resemble useful infrastructure, not speculative energy beams.

## Character Expressions

Create a minimum expression set:

1. **Welcome** — gentle smile, open posture
2. **Learning** — glasses slightly lowered, curious attention
3. **Risk Alert** — focused face, one hand raised carefully
4. **Planning** — holding a notebook or route map
5. **Teaching** — pointing to a simple lesson board
6. **Community** — sitting with workers and teachers
7. **Progress** — calm satisfaction, not victory mania
8. **Safety** — securing the hardware wallet

## Character Props

Approved:

- Ledger or unbranded hardware wallet
- Route map
- Notebook
- Pencil
- Violin case
- Music stand
- Safety cone
- Thermos
- Simple tablet showing an educational interface
- Lobster Watch safety icon

Avoid:

- Cash piles
- Gold chains
- Luxury watches
- Champagne
- Sports cars
- Rockets
- Casino objects
- Weapons
- Glowing coins
- Trading charts presented as guaranteed upward movement

## Mascot Use Levels

### Level A — Full narrative scene

Use for homepage hero, campaign key art, manifesto launches, major community events, and founder storytelling.

### Level B — Waist-up guide

Use in product onboarding, Hippo Academy lessons, Lobster Watch explanations, and social education posts.

### Level C — Head icon

Use for avatars, favicons, stickers, badges, and small UI moments.

### Level D — Symbolic silhouette

Use for one-color stamps, embroidery, workwear, print, and situations where detail is limited.

# 6. Typography

## Typography Goals

Typography should be:

- Friendly
- Highly readable on mobile
- Strong enough for workwear and transportation contexts
- Warm enough for music and education
- Clear in Traditional Chinese and English

## Recommended Open-Source System

### English and numerals

**Primary:** Atkinson Hyperlegible  
Use for body text, product interfaces, lessons, risk explanations, and accessibility-sensitive content.

**Display option:** Manrope  
Use for large headlines, section titles, campaign graphics, and logo exploration.

### Traditional Chinese

**Primary:** Noto Sans TC  
Use for body copy, navigation, products, and educational content.

**Editorial accent:** Noto Serif TC  
Use sparingly for founder quotes, manifesto excerpts, Hippo Stories titles, and music-related storytelling.

## Type Hierarchy

| Style | Recommended use |
|---|---|
| Display XL | Hero slogan and major campaign statements |
| Heading 1 | Page title |
| Heading 2 | Major section |
| Heading 3 | Product or story card |
| Body Large | Introductory and founder-led copy |
| Body | General reading and product explanations |
| Caption | Sources, timestamps, limitations, and image notes |
| Data | Risk scores, dates, and structured values |

## Typography Character

- Headlines may be confident and compact.
- Body copy should have generous line spacing.
- Chinese text should not be squeezed into overly tight line heights.
- Use sentence case in English instead of constant all caps.
- The slogan may use uppercase in selected campaign art, but not throughout the interface.

## Numeral Rules

- Use tabular numerals only when comparing structured data.
- Do not make APY, token balances, or risk scores visually larger than the explanation surrounding them.
- Always pair important numbers with context and limitations.

## Avoid

- Futuristic techno fonts for body text
- Heavy condensed fonts that resemble sports betting
- Script fonts that reduce accessibility
- Mixing more than two type families in one composition
- Oversized percentage figures used as promotional hooks

# 7. Community Voice

## Voice Principle

Baby Hippo speaks like a thoughtful founder, patient teacher, and reliable coworker.

It does not speak like:

- A financial oracle
- A loud token promoter
- A hacker persona
- A luxury lifestyle account
- A corporation hiding behind jargon

## Voice Traits

### Plainspoken

Explain unfamiliar words. Use short sentences when risk is important.

### Respectful

Never shame people for limited income, rural backgrounds, basic questions, or previous mistakes.

### Honest

State what is known, unknown, simulated, delayed, or risky.

### Encouraging

Recognize effort and learning without overstating progress.

### Founder-led

Use real experience, responsibility, and humility. “We are learning with the community” is stronger than pretending to have every answer.

### Bilingual

Traditional Chinese and English should both feel intentional. Chinese should not read like an afterthought or a machine translation.

## Core Messages

- “You do not have to know everything to begin.”
- “Understand the risk before you act.”
- “Progress can be slow and still be meaningful.”
- “Education before speculation.”
- “People are more important than the token.”
- “Tools for ordinary people, not only whales.”
- “From Worker To On-Chain Boss.”

## Example Voice Transformations

| Avoid | Baby Hippo voice |
|---|---|
| “Don't miss this opportunity.” | “Take time to understand the opportunity and its risks.” |
| “Earn passive income now.” | “Learn how lending works, including what can go wrong.” |
| “Your portfolio is failing.” | “Your portfolio may be concentrated. Here is why that matters.” |
| “Only beginners make this mistake.” | “This is a common mistake. Here is how to recognize it next time.” |
| “BHC rewards are coming!” | “Testnet BHC can recognize learning and responsible participation.” |
| “Become rich on-chain.” | “Build knowledge, safer habits, and confidence step by step.” |

## Founder Voice Formula

Founder communication can follow:

1. **Real observation:** What was seen in work, teaching, or community life?
2. **Human meaning:** Why does it matter to ordinary people?
3. **Practical lesson:** What can the community learn or do?
4. **Honest boundary:** What is not promised or not yet ready?
5. **Invitation:** How can people learn or contribute without pressure?

## Words to Use Carefully

- Wealth
- Opportunity
- Investing
- Yield
- Passive income
- Freedom
- Compounding
- Safe
- Protected

These words require context. “Long-term wealth building” should mean building knowledge, discipline, resilience, and potentially assets over time. It must never imply guaranteed financial growth.

# 8. Social Media Style

## Social Purpose

Social media should extend the community and education mission. It is not primarily a token-promotion channel.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Learn With Baby Hippo

Short explanations of:

- Wallet safety
- DCA
- DeFi lending
- Risk concentration
- Liquidation
- Scam warning signs
- Hardware-wallet habits

### 2. Lobster Watch Alerts

Educational risk examples with:

- What happened
- Why it matters
- What evidence to check
- What not to assume

Never turn a risk alert into fear-based marketing.

### 3. Hippo Stories

Consent-based stories from:

- Drivers
- Workers
- Music teachers
- Small business owners
- Rural communities
- First-time learners

The person, lesson, and future dream should matter more than financial numbers.

### 4. Founder Road Notes

Short founder reflections connecting:

- Freight and logistics
- Violin teaching
- Rural life in Miaoli
- Learning Web3
- Building Baby Hippo responsibly

### 5. Practice and Progress

Celebrate:

- A completed lesson
- A safer wallet habit
- A question asked
- A scam recognized
- A simulated DCA plan
- Useful community feedback

### 6. Music, Work, and Community

Show the full brand world:

- Violin practice
- Piano-key learning graphics
- Local landscapes
- Workday routines
- Community workshops
- Art and education events

## Recommended Content Mix

- 35% education
- 20% risk awareness
- 20% community stories
- 15% founder and behind-the-scenes building
- 10% product updates and testnet participation

Token-specific content should remain within the product-update portion and should never dominate the feed.

## Post Design System

### Educational carousel

- Cover: One plain-language question
- Slides 2–4: Simple explanation
- Slide 5: Risk or limitation
- Final slide: One calm next step

### Risk card

- Severity word and icon
- One-sentence finding
- Supporting evidence
- “Why this matters”
- Link to Academy lesson

### Hippo Story card

- Approved photo or illustration
- Member-selected name
- Occupation or community
- One direct quote
- One useful lesson
- No portfolio ranking

### Founder note

- Documentary image or simple mascot scene
- Short personal observation
- Connection to brand mission
- Honest invitation to respond

## Platform Avatar

Use the mascot head:

- Blue-gray face
- Sunglasses
- Small amber reflective-vest collar
- Deep Road Navy circular background
- Minimal detail at small size

Do not use a BHC coin symbol as the main community avatar.

## Social Templates

### Square post

- 1080 × 1080
- One headline
- One supporting illustration
- Maximum two brand accents
- Visible Baby Hippo signature

### Portrait post

- 1080 × 1350
- Strong headline in the upper third
- Story or lesson in the middle
- Source, limitation, or next step near the bottom

### Short video

- 9:16
- Captions always on
- First three seconds state the learning question
- Calm pacing
- No flashing profit figures or countdowns

## Social Caption Pattern

1. Relatable opening
2. Plain-language explanation
3. Risk or limitation
4. Practical learning step
5. Non-pressured community invitation

## Hashtag Direction

Use a small, meaningful set such as:

- `#BabyHippo`
- `#FromWorkerToOnChainBoss`
- `#鏈上成長`
- `#Web3教育`
- `#風險管理`
- `#HippoStories`

Avoid trending token, “100x,” pump, moon, or get-rich hashtags.

## Social Moderation Tone

- Answer beginner questions respectfully.
- Correct misinformation without humiliating the person.
- Remove scams and impersonation quickly.
- Never ask for seed phrases or private wallet information.
- Do not celebrate attacks, exploits, or other people's financial losses.
- Label simulations, testnet content, sponsored content, and partnerships clearly.

# Logo Direction and Concepts

## Logo Strategy

The logo must represent the whole Baby Hippo mission, not literally illustrate every part of the founder biography.

The primary mark should communicate:

- A friendly but sturdy hippo
- A road or path
- Calm forward movement
- Accessibility and trust

Music, rural scenery, the truck, and blockchain pathways should enrich the wider identity system rather than overcrowd the core mark.

## Recommended Logo Architecture

| Asset | Purpose | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Primary mark | Everyday public identity | Road Hippo symbol + Baby Hippo wordmark |
| Bilingual lockup | Taiwan and formal communication | English and Traditional Chinese names |
| Community badge | Events, workwear, patches | Five-world circular emblem |
| Product signature | Product navigation | Primary mark + product name |
| Utility icon | App, favicon, social avatar | Simplified hippo head |
| One-color stamp | Print and embroidery | Solid silhouette with minimal road detail |

## Logo Mood References

Designers should look for:

- The durability of transportation badges
- The warmth of community organizations

# Logo Usage Rules

## Clear Space

Use the height of the mascot's ear or the capital “B” as the minimum clear-space unit around the logo.

## Minimum Size

- Digital primary logo: test at 120 px wide or larger
- Icon: test at 24 px and 16 px
- Print: test at 25 mm wide or larger

Final measurements should be confirmed after the logo artwork exists.

## Approved Backgrounds

- Field Cream
- Piano Ivory
- Deep Road Navy
- Morning Sky
- Simple photographic areas with sufficient contrast

## Do Not

- Stretch or rotate the logo
- Add gold chrome or speculative coin effects
- Place flames, rockets, or price arrows behind it
- Put the mascot in luxury clothing
- Replace the hippo with a truck as the primary symbol
- Use the exact Ledger logo as part of Baby Hippo's own logo
- Add too many founder-story symbols to small logo versions

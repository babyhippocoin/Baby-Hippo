# Hippo Stories

> **People are the community. The community is more important than the token.**  
> **人就是社群，社群永遠比代幣更重要。**

## Purpose

Hippo Stories is Baby Hippo's community storytelling system.

It exists to recognize real people—not whales, influencers, celebrity traders, or accounts built around hype. We want to share the lives of ordinary people who are learning, saving, planning, managing risk, and using Web3 tools responsibly as part of a longer journey.

The purpose of Baby Hippo is not to create the next speculative meme coin. The purpose is to help ordinary people learn, grow, support one another, and improve life step by step.

Hippo Stories makes that purpose visible through human stories.

## Who We Want to Highlight

- Truck and freight drivers
- Logistics workers
- Labor workers
- Violin teachers
- Piano teachers
- Grassroots arts educators
- Small business owners
- People from rural towns and communities
- First-time Web3 learners
- Ordinary workers building a better future

A person does not need a large portfolio, dramatic success, or public following to have a meaningful story. Showing up, learning from a mistake, building an emergency fund, starting a small DCA habit, or recognizing a new risk can all represent real progress.

## Why Stories Matter

Financial and technical products often make ordinary people feel invisible. Their advertising celebrates large numbers, fast wins, famous founders, and people who already know the language.

Baby Hippo should tell a different story.

Hippo Stories can:

- Help beginners see people whose lives feel familiar.
- Turn difficult ideas into relatable experiences.
- Show that progress can be slow, uneven, and still meaningful.
- Give dignity to work that is often overlooked.
- Connect people across transportation, labor, education, small business, and rural communities.
- Remind the product team whom Baby Hippo is being built for.
- Build trust through honest journeys rather than promotional claims.

Stories are not proof that a financial strategy will work for everyone. They are personal experiences, shared so the community can learn and feel less alone.

## Story Principles

### Real People Before Big Numbers

The person, their work, and their growth matter more than portfolio size, token balance, or return percentage.

### Progress Before Performance

We celebrate learning, consistency, safer decisions, recovery from mistakes, and willingness to ask questions. We do not turn community members into competitors.

### Honesty Before Hype

A story may include uncertainty, setbacks, paused plans, or lessons learned. We do not rewrite someone's life into an overnight-success advertisement.

### Education Before Promotion

Every story should help the reader understand a habit, concept, or risk. It should never pressure the reader to buy a token, copy a strategy, or act from fear of missing out.

### Consent Before Content

The person owns their story. Publication requires clear and informed permission. They may use a nickname, hide identifying details, decline financial questions, review the final story, and ask for removal later.

### Community Before Token

BHC may recognize learning or participation, but no reward is more important than trust. A person should never feel required to share private details, promote BHC, or present a positive financial outcome to qualify for recognition.

## Standard Story Structure

Every Hippo Story can follow this simple structure:

### 1. Background

- Who is this person?
- What kind of work do they do?
- Where do they come from?
- What does an ordinary day look like?

### 2. Life Challenges

- What responsibilities or pressures are they carrying?
- What makes saving or learning difficult?
- What obstacles have they faced without exposing details they wish to keep private?

### 3. Financial Goals

- What does greater stability mean to them?
- Are they working toward an emergency fund, education, retirement, family support, a home, or a small business?
- What time horizon feels realistic?

Goals must be presented as personal intentions, not guaranteed outcomes.

### 4. Learning Journey

- What did they understand first?
- Which topics were confusing?
- What lesson, person, or experience helped?
- What would they tell another beginner?

### 5. DCA Progress

- Have they created a regular saving or simulated DCA plan?
- What amount or schedule feels sustainable, if they are comfortable sharing?
- How do they respond when life requires them to pause or adjust?

DCA is a planning habit, not a promise of profit. Exact amounts and performance figures are optional.

### 6. Risk Management Habits

- How do they protect their wallet and recovery phrase?
- How do they recognize suspicious messages or unrealistic promises?
- Do they limit exposure, avoid leverage, check data sources, or keep an emergency reserve?
- Has Lobster Watch helped them notice anything?

### 7. Future Dreams

- What kind of life are they trying to build?
- What do they hope to learn next?
- How would they like to help their family or community?

The story should end with the person's direction and hope—not a token price or financial claim.

## Story Profile Templates

## 1. Driver Story

**Suggested title:** _The Long Road, One Steady Step at a Time_

**Profile prompts**

- **Background:** What do you drive, what routes do you cover, and how long have you worked in transportation?
- **Life challenges:** How do long hours, fuel costs, irregular schedules, health, or family responsibilities affect your plans?
- **Financial goals:** What kind of stability are you working toward?
- **Learning journey:** When did you first hear about wallets, DCA, or DeFi? What was hardest to understand?
- **DCA progress:** How did you choose a plan that fits an irregular income or schedule?
- **Risk management habits:** How do you avoid scams while working on the road? What safety checks do you use?
- **Future dreams:** What would more time, stability, or knowledge make possible for you and your family?

**Story focus:** Discipline, irregular schedules, practical learning, and steady progress—not income size or investment performance.

## 2. Teacher Story

**Suggested title:** _Teaching Others While Learning Something New_

This template welcomes violin teachers, piano teachers, music educators, tutors, and grassroots arts teachers.

**Profile prompts**

- **Background:** What do you teach, whom do you teach, and what keeps you committed to education?
- **Life challenges:** How do student schedules, unstable enrollment, preparation time, and education costs affect daily life?
- **Financial goals:** Are you planning for instruments, a studio, further study, retirement, or greater income stability?
- **Learning journey:** How did you begin learning about digital assets, and how do you explain unfamiliar ideas to yourself?
- **DCA progress:** What does a patient, sustainable plan look like alongside teaching income?
- **Risk management habits:** How do you separate learning from speculation and protect yourself from persuasive online claims?
- **Future dreams:** What would you like to create for your students, family, or local arts community?

**Story focus:** Patience, education, care, and the connection between teaching others and becoming a lifelong learner.

## 3. Rural Entrepreneur Story

**Suggested title:** _Building from a Small Town_

This template welcomes shop owners, family businesses, farmers, craftspeople, service providers, and people creating opportunities outside major cities.

**Profile prompts**

- **Background:** What do you build or provide, and what is special about your town or community?
- **Life challenges:** How do limited resources, distance, seasonal demand, staffing, or access to information affect the business?
- **Financial goals:** Are you building a reserve, improving equipment, supporting family, or preparing for slower seasons?
- **Learning journey:** What made digital finance or Web3 relevant to you? Which ideas felt distant or difficult?
- **DCA progress:** How do you balance long-term planning with the cash needs of a small business?
- **Risk management habits:** How do you protect business funds, separate budgets, verify information, and avoid high-pressure opportunities?
- **Future dreams:** What would you like the business or town to look like in five or ten years?

**Story focus:** Local resilience, responsible experimentation, and creating possibility without leaving one's community behind.

## 4. First-Time Web3 Learner Story

**Suggested title:** _My First Steps On-Chain_

**Profile prompts**

- **Background:** What kind of work and life experience are you bringing into Web3?
- **Life challenges:** What made financial learning difficult or intimidating before?
- **Financial goals:** What does learning to manage money or digital assets mean to you?
- **Learning journey:** What was your first wallet experience? Which Hippo Academy lesson helped most?
- **DCA progress:** Have you made a simulated or testnet plan? What did the exercise teach you?
- **Risk management habits:** What scam warning, wallet habit, or Lobster Watch alert changed how you think?
- **Future dreams:** What would you like to understand or feel confident doing next?

**Story focus:** Beginner courage, questions, mistakes, and knowledge gained—not speed, trading activity, or profit.

## A Simple Publication Format

Each published story can use this reader-friendly format:

1. A portrait, workplace image, or illustration approved by the member.
2. A short introduction in the member's own voice.
3. The seven story sections.
4. One practical lesson learned.
5. One risk habit worth sharing.
6. One future dream.
7. A clear note that this is a personal story, not financial advice or a promised result.
8. Links to relevant Hippo Academy lessons or Baby Hippo tools.

## Connecting Hippo Stories to the Product

### Hippo Academy

- Turn common story questions into beginner lessons.
- Attach relevant lessons to each published story.
- Invite featured members to share one thing they wish they had known earlier.
- Create learning paths such as “Driver's First Wallet,” “Teacher's DCA Basics,” or “Small Business Risk Fundamentals.”
- Use anonymized story themes to improve explanations without exposing personal information.

Stories give Academy lessons a human reason to exist; Academy gives stories practical next steps.

### Lobster Watch

- Let members explain how they learned to recognize concentration, suspicious tokens, stale data, or lending risk.
- Link each risk-management habit to an educational Lobster Watch example.
- Allow members to discuss a warning without publishing their wallet address or exact holdings.
- Use recurring confusion from interviews to improve alert language.

Lobster Watch should protect people. Hippo Stories should help the community understand why that protection matters.

### BHC Rewards

Testnet BHC may recognize:

- Completing a story-related Academy lesson.
- Submitting a thoughtful story nomination.
- Participating in an interview with informed consent.
- Reviewing a draft for accuracy.
- Contributing a useful safety lesson or community reflection.

BHC must not be payment for exaggerated success, promotion, recruitment, wallet disclosure, or positive claims about Baby Hippo. A member may tell their story without accepting a reward, and declining publication must never affect their standing in the community.

### Community Events

Hippo Stories can support:

- Small online or local storytelling circles.
- Driver, worker, teacher, and rural entrepreneur community nights.
- Beginner question sessions with no trading pressure.
- Scam-awareness workshops built around anonymized experiences.
- “What I learned this month” gatherings.
- Music and arts community events that connect creativity with long-term learning.
- Annual community collections celebrating steady progress.

Events should favor conversation and practical learning over token promotion, price discussion, or competitive portfolio displays.

## Story Nomination and Review Flow

1. **Nominate:** A member nominates themselves or someone else with permission.
2. **Invite:** The team explains the purpose, privacy choices, and optional reward.
3. **Listen:** A trained interviewer follows the appropriate template without pressuring for financial details.
4. **Draft:** The story is written in warm, plain language.
5. **Review:** The featured member checks names, facts, tone, images, and details.
6. **Consent:** Publication approval is recorded clearly.
7. **Publish:** The story includes educational links and a no-promises note.
8. **Follow up:** The member can correct, update, anonymize, or request removal.

## Safety, Dignity, and Privacy

- Never publish a wallet address, seed phrase, private key, identity document, exact home address, or account credential.
- Avoid exact asset balances unless the member independently requests disclosure and the team confirms the risk.
- Do not ask someone to connect a wallet to prove their story.
- Do not expose debts, family matters, immigration status, health information, or income without explicit informed consent.
- Children and students require special protection and should not be identifiable without appropriate guardian permission and safeguarding review.
- Avoid language that presents hardship as entertainment or turns a person into a marketing symbol.
- Clearly identify fictional examples, composites, illustrations, and anonymized stories.
- Give featured members a practical way to request changes or removal.

## Editorial Checklist

Before publication, confirm:

- Is the person—not the token—the center of the story?
- Does the member recognize themselves in the final version?
- Is their consent informed, specific, and recorded?
- Have unnecessary private or financially sensitive details been removed?
- Does the story avoid guaranteed outcomes and implied financial promises?
- Does it avoid glorifying leverage, exploitation, scams, or pump-and-dump behavior?
- Is any setback described with dignity?
- Is the useful lesson clear?
- Are Academy or Lobster Watch links relevant rather than promotional?
- Would we still publish this story if BHC did not exist?

If the answer to the last question is no, the story does not yet reflect Baby Hippo's values.
